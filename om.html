A Resonance Instrument

Calm you can
hold in your hands

A hand-turned hardwood resonator that rests on your chest during breathwork, meditation, or chanting. It receives the vibration your body makes and returns it as something deeper — tuned to frequencies shown to quiet the mind and settle the nervous system. It smells like walnut. It feels like holding nature in your hands. And it will never need charging.

How It Works

Hum, chant, or tone

You vocalize

Hum, chant Om, or sustain a tone. Your chest wall vibrates — the egg rests on it and receives that vibration.

Wood responds

The hollow body catches your vibration and amplifies it acoustically, the way a cello body resonates from a bowed string.

You feel it back

The resonance returns through the flat base into your sternum — a distinct sensation that your voice alone doesn't produce.

500
years without recharging

There are no circuits, no batteries, and no firmware. The construction is so simple that there's nothing to wear out or break down.

Finding your frequency
Tuning

Twist to tune

The egg's two hand-turned halves rotate against each other to adjust internal tension, changing which frequencies it responds to most. You set a target, whether it's 111 Hz or Om near 136 Hz. Then vocalize and twist slowly until you feel it.

The Research

Why chest vibration at
specific frequencies matters

111 Hz

The frequency of ancient stone

Neolithic chambers across Europe resonate in a narrow band averaging 111 Hz. MRI studies show this frequency quiets the prefrontal cortex and language centers, inducing alpha-state patterns associated with deep meditation.

Cambridge & Princeton archaeoacoustics, 1996
Om

Vagus nerve stimulation through voice

fMRI imaging found that Om chanting deactivated the amygdala and hippocampus — the same areas quieted by implanted vagus nerve stimulators used in depression treatment. The difference was vibration transmitted through the chest to the vagus nerve.

Kalyani et al., Int. Journal of Yoga, 2011
HRV

Parasympathetic shift

Slow exhalation during chanting shifts the nervous system toward rest-and-digest. Regular practice improves heart rate variability — a central biomarker for stress resilience.

Multiple studies, Int. Journal of Yoga
40 Hz

Gamma entrainment

Gamma-frequency stimulation entrains neural oscillations and promotes neuroplasticity. Vibration through the sternum offers a tactile pathway that complements auditory and visual entrainment.

Iaccarino et al., Nature, 2016
